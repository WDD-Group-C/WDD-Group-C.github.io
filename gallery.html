Reduce Marine Pollution

Marine pollution is the combination of chemicals and trash humans make,
which are blown or thrown into the ocean.
While that is a solution to get rid of trash on land,
it threatens the lives of marine life and therefore, needs to be reduced.
To fulfill the act, the following steps could be taken;
  • Stop the usage of plastic bags (Use reusable bags and bottles instead)
  • Get together as a community to clean rivers and beaches
  • Avoid littering
  • Proper disposal of trash



Protect and Restore Ecosystems

People are regularly damaging the ecosystem, but to save marine life,
it is essential to restore the damages that have already been done
and make an effort to prevent the damages.
The following steps could be taken to protect and restore the ecosystem;
  • Establishment of national parks, marine sanctuaries, and many more...
  • Regulating laws and regulations to preserve the mentioned areas.
  • Encouraging to plant native trees in deforested
    areas to restore forest ecosystems



Protect and Restore Ecosystems

Ocean acidification occurs when the PH value decreases for a long period,
caused by the intake of carbon dioxide (CO)2.
this poses threats to the coral reefs, shellfish and various other marine organisms.
Following are some strategies to prevent ocean acidification;
  • Increasing the use of solar winds and hydroelectric power
  • Promoting energy efficiency
  • Reducing the reliance on fossil fuels

Sustainable fishing is a good approach as it respects marine systems
by adapting to the natural reproductive rates of fish, maintains a balance,
and ensures their survival. These methods help avoid capturing species
that do not contain commercial value.



Protect and Restore Ecosystems

Conserving the coastal and marine areas is crucial to ensure the health of the marine ecosystems.
Additionally, it is vital to maintain biodiversity and sustain fisheries.
Following are some ways to conserve coastal and marine areas
  • Create zones within the MPA to prohibit all extractive activities.
  • Strengthen regulations to prevent oil spills and reduce their impact
  • Save mangrove forests



Protect and Restore Ecosystems

By ending subsidies that contribute to overfishing, we can ensure long-term health
of marine ecosystems and the communities that are dependent on them.
Following are some ways to do so
  • Increase the number of individuals who monitor
    fishing activities and enforce laws related to subsidy reforms
  • Collaborate with national parties to develop
    coordinated strategies to reduce overfishing
  • Provide fishers with training to adapt to sustainable practices
